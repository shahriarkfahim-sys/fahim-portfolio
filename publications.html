Publications

Md. Shahriar Kabir Fahim's research focuses on communication disorders, disability inclusion, and social development in Bangladesh. His work spans clinical speech-language pathology, augmentative and alternative communication (AAC), and disaster response for persons with disabilities, grounded in fieldwork across Bangladesh.

Research Interests

  • — Developmental Language Disorders (DLD) — awareness and attitudes
  • — Screen time and speech-language development in children
  • — Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) practices in Bangladesh
  • — Disability-inclusive disaster response and humanitarian action
  • — Communication access for persons with disabilities in low-resource settings
  • — Youth mobilisation and social innovation in development contexts

Current Research Projects

  • (Under review) Fahim SK. Association between Screen Time and Articulation Difficulty among Children in Bangladesh: A Comparative Study between Typical and Atypical Children. Submitted for journal review.
  • (Ongoing) Fahim SK. Disability-Inclusive Disaster Response: Examining the Experiences of Persons with Disabilities During Cyclone Remal in Khulna, Bangladesh.
  • (Ongoing) Fahim SK. AAC Recommendation Practices among Speech-Language Pathologists in Urban Areas of Bangladesh.

A growing body of published research and public writing on communication disorders, disability, and social development. Fahim's peer-reviewed work appears in international journals, alongside essays and book summaries at shahriar-fahim.com.
